12 Bars Blues

Following a musician with a broken heart through the streets of Río Piedras - or how the musician Agustín Muñoz transports us to the Río Piedras of a decade ago.

By María de los Milagros Colón Cruz

It's 2011, and the jangueo begins and ends in Río Piedras, a vibrant university town with an incredible nightlife. Students walk up and down the streets with a beer in hand. Freestylers get ready to battle with improvised rhymes on a street corner. Someone finishes a mural under spotlights in the Ponce de León Avenue. I put on my boots and apply the eyeliner. Meanwhile, a musician with a broken heart and his guitar gets to that place where everyone knows the night starts.

Let him and his guitar tell his story. The night will be long...

It always starts with a shot of chichaíto in El Refugio. The mix of rum and anise burns any throat and speeds up the night in Río Piedras, in the Puerto Rican capital.

Our friend gets there and leave as soon as his past love appears with her new love. It is, indeed, a small town. A broken heart is the best excuse to go to the next bar: El Boricua.

'As I had at hand only my guitar,to the sound of whiskey I wrote the blues of the twelve bars.'

But she follows him. Or so he thinks. The truth is everyone goes to El Boricua after closing El Refugio. He runs to El Taller and then moves to la Greca, but it is closed. He ends up in Café Borikén, with its cheap beers and sweaty perreo (reggaetón dancing). He dances, of course. And cries, of course.

Already on Universidad Avenue, he moves to El 8 de Blanco. He knows it's the classic route. Maybe he's not trying to get away from her and, sure enough, there she is. At this point, it's difficult to know if it's the alcohol, the heat, the grief, or the rage, but things are getting intense. Better move to El Vidy's.

'Me in La Tripleta y el Pincho, your [boyfriend] there in Le Château... If they don't hold me, I form here a titingó'

It's okay. That titingó, or chaos, is probably not getting physical. Our friend is facing this whole drama just with the 12 Bars Blues

I can see him wobbling his way out of the hubbub where the youngster party.

'On the way to El Confetti, after a stop at El Dug Out, the whiskey is already going to win by knockout

The sun comes out, and the streets smell of alcohol. Nobody can stand him anymore after his route through 12 bars. They kick him out of the bar.

After many chords, whiskies, and walks, El Blues de las 12 Barras ends.

The 12 Bar Blues is not only the story of a broken heart. It is also a journey through the nostalgia of a generation that fueled Río Piedras with life and magic, art and dreams, and even the desire to build a new country. A decade, two hurricanes, several earthquakes, and a pandemic later, the town seems like another place. The artist, Agustín Muñoz Ríos, attests this in a new song that has not been released.

About the artist

Muñoz Díaz has cultivated Nueva Trova and related genres (bolero, bossa nova, blues, Latin American folk song) since 2000. Apart from his solo work, he has participated in various groups, especially in the plena, batucada, bolero and Cuban son genres. He is a founding member of the Spanish rock group Perros de Pavlov, with two musical productions (Salivando in 2011 and En la calle in 2014), being the author of most of their songs.
